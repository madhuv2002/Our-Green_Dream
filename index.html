Fact Check #1

Although over a hundred million tonnes of plastic is produced globally every year, only 10% of this is recycled, and around 7 million tonnes of the plastic that is simply trashed ends up in the world's oceans.

Fact Check #2

Food wastes make up the largest portion of garbage thrown out by any household, amounting to over 7.2 million tonnes in a year - more than half of which is still edible and could instead be a source of nourishment for thousands of people.

Fact Check #3

The Pacific Ocean Garbage Patch is a garbage island found in the ocean. It is made up of 7 million tonnes of garbage, and is nearly twice the size of Texas - it is the largest garbage patch in the world.

Check out these green alternatives

Plastic Straws

Every plastic straw that has ever been produced still exists on our planet today. Whether it be in oceans or landfills, the amount of waste that is created by plastic straws alone is enough to circle around the planet twice. Many countries have begun banning or taxing the use of plastic straws, and it's all for the best.

Plastic Utensils

From fast food restaurants to school lunches, plastic utensils are used by millions of people every single day. Without even realizing it, many use plastic utensils 2-3 times a day. Click here to learn more about why they're bad for the environment and find out what you can do to lessen the impact that plastic utensils have on our planet.

Produce Bags

On average, a family of four goes through 60 plastic grocery bags in less than a month. Sure, pastic bags have many household and otherwise functional uses, but most of them are thrown directly in the trash, leaving only one in 200 bags recycled by the average person.

Paper/Plastic Cups

Every trip to your favorite coffee shop or fast food restaurant is accompanied by the use of a plastic cup or an unrecyclable paper cup. The ones that can be recylced are often simply thrown away due to convenience, and most companies fail to realize that this habit is wreaking havoc on our planet.

Resealable Plastic Bags

Whether you're packing your lunch or storing leftover snacks, plastic bags may be one of the plastic-based items that you use the most frequently. Like with most other plastic goods, resealable plastic bags are often not recycled and are used only once by most consumers.

Plastic Toothbrushes

As a necessity to maintain hygiene and stay healthy, plastic toothbrushes are consistently used at a alarmingly high rate. Once thrown away, toothbrushes sit in landfills for years yet won't ever decompose correctly. Although it may not seem too harmful, the waste created by toothbrushes alone is enough to scare any qualified dentist.

Plastic Grocery Bags

Every grocery store offers bags to place produce in prior to placing it into your cart or basket. A single person can use nearly 10 produce bags in a single visit to the grocery store, not to mention the amount of plastic that can be found in the rest of their cart. Luckily, there are simple ways to replace produce bags and help keep our planet looking beautiful!

Plastic Water Bottles

Plastic water bottles are the main contributor to the amount of plastic waste on our planet. They are sold on every street corner and can be found in most homes, schools, and offices around the country. Although they may seem more convenient, they are incredibly expensive and detrimental to our planet. Click here to learn more about how you can save money and plastic while also saving the planet!
